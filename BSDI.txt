1. Executive Summary

The Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer (BSD) Conjecture is one of the seven Clay Millennium Prize Problems and stands as the deepest unsolved problem at the intersection of number theory, algebraic geometry, and complex analysis. It asserts that the rank of the MordellвҖ“Weil group of an elliptic curve вҖ” the number of independent rational solutions вҖ” is exactly the order of vanishing of the curve's L-function at the critical point s = 1.

All existing proof attempts are opinionated: they assume a rank, choose a preferred prime, impose a cohomology theory, or rely on a specific geometric structure. They function for rank 0 and rank 1. For rank вүҘ 2, essentially nothing is proved.

This proposal introduces a fundamentally different strategy: the construction of an agnostic decoder вҖ” a mathematical tool that recovers the MordellвҖ“Weil group directly from the L-function signal without any prior knowledge of rank or structure. The decoder is premised on a single observation emerging from this research programme:

BSD is not a conjecture about elliptic curves. It is a conjecture about whether arithmetic encoding is lossless.

The topology distributes. The L-function encodes. The decoder must recover. Proving lossless recovery IS the proof of BSD.
