1. The Encoding Pipeline

The encoding path from rational points to the L-function is a sequence of structure-preserving maps:

$$E(\mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Z}^r \oplus T$$

where $r$ is the Mordell–Weil rank and $T$ is the finite torsion subgroup.

1.1 Topology Distribution

The winding structure map embeds rational points into the first integral cohomology of the torus:

$$E(\mathbb{Q}) \hookrightarrow H^1(E, \mathbb{Z})$$

Independent rational generators $P_1, \ldots, P_r \in E(\mathbb{Q})$ correspond to independent winding classes $[\gamma_1], \ldots, [\gamma_r] \in H^1(E, \mathbb{Z})$.

1.2 Reduction and Local Counts

For each prime $p$ of good reduction, the local point count is:

$$N_p = |E(\mathbb{F}_p)|$$

The local Euler factor at a prime $p$ of good reduction is:

$$L_p(E, s) = \left(1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{1-2s}\right)^{-1}$$

where $a_p = p + 1 - N_p$.

1.3 The L-Function (Euler Product)

The global L-function is assembled as an Euler product over all primes:

$$L(E, s) = \prod_{p \nmid N} \left(1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{1-2s}\right)^{-1} \cdot \prod_{p \mid N} \left(1 - a_p p^{-s}\right)^{-1}$$

By the Modularity Theorem (Wiles–Taylor–Wiles), $L(E,s)$ admits analytic continuation to all of $\mathbb{C}$ and satisfies a functional equation.

2. The BSD Conjecture: Formal Statement

2.1 Rank Equality (Weak BSD)

$$\mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E, s) = \mathrm{rank}, E(\mathbb{Q})$$

2.2 Leading Term Formula (Strong BSD)

Let $r = \mathrm{rank}, E(\mathbb{Q})$. Define the leading coefficient:

$$L^*(E, 1) = \lim_{s \to 1} \frac{L(E,s)}{(s-1)^r}$$

The strong BSD conjecture asserts:

$$\boxed{L^*(E, 1) = \frac{\Omega_E \cdot R_E \cdot |\Sha(E/\mathbb{Q})| \cdot \prod_p c_p}{|T|^2}}$$

3. Motivic Cohomology: The Pre-Encoding Structure

3.1 Motivic Cohomology Group

The relevant motivic cohomology group for an elliptic curve $E/\mathbb{Q}$ is:

$$H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E,, \mathbb{Z}(1))$$

This is the structure at the motivic origin — prior to any realisation (no choice of prime $\ell$, no embedding into $\mathbb{C}$, no Euler system direction). For an elliptic curve of rank $r$:

$$H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R} \cong \mathbb{R}^r$$

Independent rational generators $P_1, \ldots, P_r$ correspond to orthogonal basis vectors $\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_r$ in this group — they do not interfere.

3.2 Deligne Cohomology

The boundary structure (analytic destination) is encoded in Deligne cohomology:

$$H^1_{\mathcal{D}}(E/\mathbb{R},, \mathbb{R}(1))$$

This receives the image of motivic cohomology under the regulator map and connects to the L-function's behaviour at $s = 1$.

4. The Beilinson Regulator: The Encoding Map

4.1 The Regulator Map

The Beilinson regulator is the encoding map in the holographic correspondence:

$$\mathrm{reg}: H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R} ;\longrightarrow; H^1_{\mathcal{D}}(E/\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{R}(1))$$

Beilinson's conjecture asserts that this map is an isomorphism (after tensoring with $\mathbb{R}$):

$$\mathrm{reg} \text{ is an isomorphism} \iff \text{the encoding is lossless}$$

4.2 Regulator as Volume

The regulator $R_E$ appearing in the BSD formula is the volume of the motivic lattice image:

$$R_E = \mathrm{vol}!\left(\mathrm{reg}!\left(H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1))\right)\right) = \det!\left(\langle P_i, P_j\rangle_{\mathrm{NT}}\right)$$

For rank $r$, the Néron–Tate height pairing matrix is:

$$\mathcal{H} = \begin{pmatrix} \langle P_1, P_1\rangle & \langle P_1, P_2\rangle & \cdots & \langle P_1, P_r\rangle \ \langle P_2, P_1\rangle & \langle P_2, P_2\rangle & \cdots & \langle P_2, P_r\rangle \ \vdots & & \ddots & \vdots \ \langle P_r, P_1\rangle & \langle P_r, P_2\rangle & \cdots & \langle P_r, P_r\rangle \end{pmatrix}$$

$$R_E = \det(\mathcal{H})$$

5. Construction B: The Motivic Inverse (Adelic Reversal Map)

5.1 The Motivic Inverse Map

The central novel object of the proposal is the motivic inverse:

$$\mathrm{reg}^{-1}: H^1_{\mathcal{D}}(E/\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{R}(1)) ;\longrightarrow; H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R}$$

This exists as an isomorphism if and only if Beilinson's conjecture holds.

5.2 Construction B as a Composition

The full adelic reversal map $\mathcal{R}$ (Construction B) is a three-step composition:

$$\mathcal{R}:; \underbrace{L\text{-function data at } s=1}{\text{analytic input}} ;\xrightarrow{;\phi_1;}; H^1{\mathcal{D}}(E/\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{R}(1)) ;\xrightarrow{;\mathrm{reg}^{-1};}; H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R} ;\xrightarrow{;\phi_3;}; E(\mathbb{Q}) \otimes \mathbb{R}$$

where:

$\phi_1$: Extraction map — reads Deligne cohomological data from $L^*(E,1)$ without rank assumption

$\mathrm{reg}^{-1}$: Motivic reconstruction — inverts the Beilinson regulator

$\phi_3$: Comparison isomorphism — natural isomorphism between motivic cohomology and the Mordell–Weil group tensored with $\mathbb{R}$ (Construction D, natural transformation $\eta$)

5.3 The Comparison Isomorphism (Construction D)

The natural transformation $\eta$ required as the third step:

$$\eta: H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R} ;\xrightarrow{;\sim;}; E(\mathbb{Q}) \otimes \mathbb{R}$$

BSD asserts this is an isomorphism. The rank $r$ is recoverable as:

$$r = \dim_{\mathbb{R}}!\left(H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R}\right) = \mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s)$$

6. The Holographic Correspondence Diagram

The complete holographic correspondence — BSD as a commutative diagram:

$$\begin{array}{ccccc} H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R} & \xrightarrow{\quad\mathrm{reg}\quad} & H^1_{\mathcal{D}}(E/\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{R}(1)) \[8pt] \Big\downarrow\scriptstyle{\eta} & & \Big\downarrow\scriptstyle{L\text{-function}} \[8pt] E(\mathbb{Q}) \otimes \mathbb{R} & \xrightarrow{\quad\text{BSD formula}\quad} & \dfrac{L^*(E,1)}{\Omega_E \cdot \prod_p c_p \cdot |T|^{-2}} \end{array}$$

The round trip $\mathcal{R}$ goes from right to left along the bottom row, closing the diagram:

$$E(\mathbb{Q}) \otimes \mathbb{R} \xleftarrow{\quad\mathcal{R} ;:; \text{Construction B}\quad} \frac{L^*(E,1)}{\Omega_E \cdot \prod_p c_p \cdot |T|^{-2}}$$

BSD is equivalent to: both horizontal arrows are isomorphisms and the diagram commutes.

7. The Tate–Shafarevich Group as a Cohomological Kernel

7.1 Classical Definition

$$\Sha(E/\mathbb{Q}) = \ker!\left(H^1(\mathbb{Q}, E) \longrightarrow \prod_{v} H^1(\mathbb{Q}_v, E)\right)$$

where the product runs over all places $v$ of $\mathbb{Q}$ (all primes $p$ and the archimedean place $\infty$).

7.2 Motivic Interpretation

At the motivic level, $\Sha$ appears as the kernel of the comparison map between motivic and étale cohomology:

$$\Sha(E/\mathbb{Q}) \cong \ker!\left(H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{Q}\ell ;\longrightarrow; H^1{\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}(E_{\bar{\mathbb{Q}}}, \mathbb{Q}\ell)^{G{\mathbb{Q}}}\right)$$

Finiteness of $\Sha$ is then equivalent to this comparison map having finite kernel:

$$|\Sha(E/\mathbb{Q})| < \infty \iff \ker(\text{comparison map}) \text{ is finite}$$

8. The Interference Pattern Problem (Rank ≥ 2)

8.1 Vanishing at s = 1 for Higher Rank

For a curve of rank $r \ge 2$, the $L$-function vanishes to order $r$ at $s=1$:

$$L(E,s) = c_r (s-1)^r + c_{r+1}(s-1)^{r+1} + \cdots, \quad c_r = L^*(E,1) \ne 0$$

The $r$ independent rational generators $P_1, \ldots, P_r$ each contribute independently to this zero. At the adelic level, their contributions interfere in the Euler product — at each prime $p$, contributions of all $r$ generators are compressed together and cannot be separated without knowing $r$ in advance.

8.2 Motivic Separation

At the motivic level, the $r$ generators correspond to orthogonal lattice directions. For independent generators:

$$\langle \xi_i, \xi_j \rangle_{\mathrm{NT}} = 0 \quad \text{for } i \ne j$$

The motivic cohomology lattice is:

$$\Lambda_E = \mathrm{reg}!\left(H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1))\right) \subset H^1_{\mathcal{D}}(E/\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{R}(1)) \cong \mathbb{R}^r$$

with:

$$R_E = \mathrm{vol}(\mathbb{R}^r / \Lambda_E)$$

There is no interference at this level: independent winding classes are simply distinct orthogonal directions in $\mathbb{R}^r$.

9. Newton Polygon Separability

9.1 The Newton Polygon

For the local L-factor at prime $p$:

$$L_p(E, s) = 1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{1-2s}$$

The Newton polygon $\mathrm{NP}_p$ records the $p$-adic valuations of coefficients. For rank $r \ge 2$, simultaneous vanishing from multiple generators creates overlapping slopes.

9.2 Separability Condition

The Newton polygon separability condition requires that for independent generators $P_1, \ldots, P_r$:

$$\mathrm{NP}p!\left(\prod{i=1}^r \phi_i(s)\right) = \sum_{i=1}^r \mathrm{NP}_p(\phi_i(s))$$

where $\phi_i(s)$ is the local contribution of the $i$-th generator. This additivity holds if and only if the generators are motivically orthogonal.

11. Proof Strategy: Formal Requirements

BSD is equivalent to proving the following three statements:

Statement 1 — Beilinson Isomorphism:

$$\mathrm{reg}: H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R} \xrightarrow{;\sim;} H^1_{\mathcal{D}}(E/\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{R}(1))$$

Statement 2 — Extraction Map Well-Definedness:

$$\phi_1: L^*(E,1) ;\longmapsto; H^1_{\mathcal{D}}(E/\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{R}(1)) \quad \text{well-defined, without rank assumption}$$

Statement 3 — Diagram Commutativity:

$$\phi_3 \circ \mathrm{reg}^{-1} \circ \phi_1 = \mathcal{R}, \quad \text{and the full holographic diagram commutes}$$

Corollary (BSD): If Statements 1–3 hold, then:

$$\mathrm{rank}, E(\mathbb{Q}) = \dim_{\mathbb{R}} H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{R} = \mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s)$$

12. Relation to Bloch–Kato

The Bloch–Kato conjecture generalises Beilinson to $p$-adic coefficients. For each prime $\ell$:

$$\mathrm{ord}{s=1} L(E,s) = \dim{\mathbb{Q}\ell} H^1_f(\mathbb{Q}, V\ell(E)) - \dim_{\mathbb{Q}\ell} H^0(\mathbb{Q}, V\ell(E))$$

where $V_\ell(E) = T_\ell(E) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}\ell} \mathbb{Q}\ell$ is the $\ell$-adic Tate module and $H^1_f$ is the Selmer (finite-part) cohomology.

In the motivic framework, Construction A (reverse motivic spectral sequence) would imply Bloch–Kato as a special case:

$$H^1_f(\mathbb{Q}, V_\ell(E)) \cong H^1_{\mathrm{mot}}(E, \mathbb{Z}(1)) \otimes \mathbb{Q}_\ell$$

Summary

The proposal reduces the BSD conjecture to three interlocking problems at the motivic level:

$$\boxed{\mathrm{BSD} \iff \bigl(\mathrm{reg ;is; an; isomorphism}\bigr) ;\wedge; \bigl(\phi_1 ;\text{well-defined}\bigr) ;\wedge; \bigl(\text{diagram commutes}\bigr)}$$

The key structural insight is that the rank $r$ is not an input to the motivic reconstruction — it is an output: the dimension of the motivic cohomology lattice recovered by $\mathcal{R}$.

$$r = \dim_\mathbb{R}(\Lambda_E) = \mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s) \quad \text{(output of } \mathcal{R}\text{, not assumption)}$$

The interference pattern that defeats existing rank $\ge 2$ tools is an artifact of operating at the adelic level; at the motivic level, independent winding classes are simply orthogonal basis vectors, already separated by the structure that generates them.
